# Manual Process: Adding Affiliate Products to Faceless YouTube Videos

*(Kid/team-friendly – fits perfectly into your AI Governance – Authorization video workflow. Keeps it educational and honest.)*

**Goal**: Earn money by recommending helpful AI tools/books/courses (e.g., AI voice tools, writing tools, or learning platforms) without changing the video much. Viewers click links in the description if they want them. **Always disclose** that you earn a small commission.

**Super Important Rules (Follow These or Don’t Do It)**

- Be honest: Only recommend tools you (or the team) think are actually good.
- Tell viewers: “Some links are affiliate links – we may earn a commission if you buy, at no extra cost to you.”
- Start small: Use free or easy affiliate programs (Amazon Associates, Grammarly, Canva Pro, ElevenLabs, etc.).
- YouTube Partner Program helps with cards/end screens, but description links work for everyone.
- Never make the video a hard sales pitch – keep it educational.

**Super Simple 6-Step Process to Mix Affiliates In**

**Step 1: Choose 1–2 Helpful Products (During Script Writing – Step 2 of Video Process)**

- In the script, naturally mention a tool that fits the topic.
Example for AI Authorization video:
    - “To learn more about AI tools like this, we like [Tool Name] for creating educational videos.”
    - Or recommend a beginner AI course/book.
- Research free affiliate programs:
    - Amazon (books/courses)
    - Grammarly, Canva, ElevenLabs, or other AI tools (search “AI tool affiliate program”).
- Sign up (adult help for accounts). Get your unique affiliate link. Test it works.

**Step 2: Mention It Naturally in the Video (During Recording/Voiceover)**

- Add 1 short line: “Check the description for a helpful tool we recommend.”
- Or verbally: “One tool that helps with AI explanations is [Tool] – link below.”
- Keep it quick – don’t interrupt the education.

**Step 3: Update the Video Description (Pre-Launch Step)**

Use this easy template (copy-paste and fill in):

Disclosure: This video contains affiliate links. If you buy through them, we may earn a small commission at no extra cost to you. Thanks for supporting us!

🔗 Recommended Tool for [Topic]: [Product Name] - [Your Affiliate Link]

[First 1-2 lines of your original description + hook]

📌 Timestamps:

0:00 Intro

...

More resources and full description here...

- Put the affiliate link in the **very first lines** (visible without clicking “Show more”).
- Add more links if you have them.

**Step 4: Add Cards & End Screen (If Your Channel Qualifies)**

- In YouTube Studio → Editor: Add a Card during the mention (links to your affiliate or a link-in-bio page).
- End Screen: Add a button linking to your website or directly to the product (needs YouTube Partner Program + verified site).
- Simple text: “Learn More About This Tool”.

**Step 5: Pin a Comment**

- After publishing, pin a comment:
“🔗 Recommended Tool here: [short link or full]. Disclosure: Affiliate link – helps support educational videos like this!”

**Step 6: Track & Review (Post-Launch)**

- Check YouTube Analytics for clicks (limited info).
- Use affiliate dashboard to see sales.
- Journal: Did viewers like the recommendation? Adjust for next video.
- Save links in your D:\Files\Output folder for reuse.

**Skills vs Tools Matching (Simple Version)**

**Skills needed:**

- Finding good tools that match the video topic
- Honest recommending (no pressure)
- Writing clear descriptions
- Following disclosure rules

**Tools (Free/Easy):**

- Affiliate programs: Amazon, Impact, PartnerStack, or direct AI tool sites
- Canva: For thumbnails mentioning the tool
- YouTube Studio: For description, cards, end screens
- Bitly (optional): Shorten links to look clean

**How to Run This with a Team of Kids**

1. **Roles** (add to existing video roles):
    - Researcher: Finds 1–2 good affiliate products.
    - Writer: Adds mention to script + disclosure to description.
    - Uploader: Adds links/cards in Studio.
    - Reviewer: Checks everything is honest and clear.
2. Use the checklist above every video. Start with **one** affiliate per video.
3. **Practice**: Do one test video with a safe affiliate (like a free tool signup) before real money.

**Why This Works for Faceless Educational Videos**

- Doesn’t change the main content – stays educational.
- Natural fit for AI Governance (recommend AI learning tools, voiceover software, etc.).
- Passive income: Old videos keep earning.
- Builds trust: Honest + helpful = more views long-term.

**Safety Note**: Get adult supervision for accounts, taxes, and YouTube rules. Focus on learning first – revenue comes later.
