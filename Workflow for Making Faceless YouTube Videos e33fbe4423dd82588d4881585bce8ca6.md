# Workflow for Making Faceless YouTube Videos

*(Designed so a team of 10-year-olds can follow it step-by-step and actually finish videos)*

**Goal**

Create a **2–5 minute faceless video** about **AI Governance – Authorization** that educates people.

No one appears on camera. Use pictures, text, simple animations, and voice.

**The Super Simple 7-Step Process**

**Step 1: Get the Instructions (The “Request”)**

- Put the handwritten note (or typed version) into a folder called D:\Files\Instructions (or just a folder called “Instructions” on your computer).
- One kid reads it out loud so everyone understands:
    - Topic = AI Governance
    - Message = Authorization
    - Length = 2–5 minutes
    - Goal = Educate

**Step 2: Research & Write the Script (The Story)**

- Use simple tools: Google Docs, Microsoft Word, or even paper + pen.
- Answer these easy questions:
    1. What is AI Authorization? (like a lock on a door)
    2. Why does it matter?
    3. Good example + Bad example
    4. One big idea kids can remember
- Keep the script **short** — aim for 250–400 words (takes about 2–4 minutes to speak).
- Mark places for pictures: “Show a lock”, “Show a computer saying YES/NO”, etc.

**Step 3: Gather Visuals (The Pictures & Clips)**

- Make a folder called D:\Files\Visuals.
- Find free pictures and short clips:
    - Use **Pixabay**, **Pexels**, **Unsplash** (no copyright problems).
    - Search for: lock, shield, computer, robot, key, approval stamp, etc.
- Save 10–20 images + any short free video clips.
- Optional: Draw simple pictures in Canva or Paint.

**Step 4: Make Voiceover (The Talking)**

**Easiest way for kids:**

- Use a free AI voice tool (ElevenLabs free tier, Google TTS, or Microsoft Edge Read Aloud).
- Or record your own voice with phone voice memos / Audacity (free).
- Speak clearly and slowly like you’re teaching a friend.

**Step 5: Build the Video (Put It All Together)**

Use one of these **kid-friendly tools** (pick **one**):

| **Tool** | **Difficulty** | **Best For** | **Why Kids Can Use It** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Canva** | Very Easy | Beginners | Drag & drop, templates, free |
| **CapCut** | Easy | Phone or Computer | TikTok-style, auto captions |
| **PowerPoint** | Easy | School computers | Export as video |
| **DaVinci Resolve** (free) | Medium | Better quality | Once they practice |

**In the chosen tool:**

- Put pictures/clips in order following the script.
- Add the voiceover.
- Add big text on screen (so people can read if sound is off).
- Add simple transitions and background music (free from YouTube Audio Library).

**Step 6: Finish & Check (The Output Folder)**

- Export the video as MP4.
- Save it in D:\Files\Output\Completed.
- Watch it together and answer:
    - Is it under 5 minutes?
    - Is the message clear?
    - Does it teach something?

**Step 7: Upload & Share**

- Upload to YouTube (with adult help for account).
- Write a simple title and description.

**Skills vs Tools Matching (Simple Version)**

**Skills needed:**

- Reading instructions
- Writing short stories/scripts
- Finding pictures
- Speaking clearly (or using AI voice)
- Putting pictures + sound together
- Checking your work

**Tools (pick simple ones):**

- Canva or CapCut (main video maker)
- Google/Pixabay (pictures)
- Phone or Audacity (voice)
- YouTube (upload)

**How to Run This with a Team of Kids**

1. **Roles** (rotate each video):
    - Researcher/Writer
    - Picture Hunter
    - Voice Person or AI Manager
    - Video Builder
    - Checker (watches and gives feedback)
2. Use a big checklist on the wall with the 7 steps. Kids check off each step with stickers.
3. Do **one short practice video** first (maybe 1 minute) before doing the AI Governance one.
4. Keep a “Done” folder so they see progress.

**Why This Works for 10-Year-Olds**

- Every step is **visual and hands-on**.
- Very few decisions — mostly follow the checklist.
- Tools are free and drag-and-drop.
- They can see their video at the end of the day.
- You can scale it: first video takes a few hours, later ones get faster.
