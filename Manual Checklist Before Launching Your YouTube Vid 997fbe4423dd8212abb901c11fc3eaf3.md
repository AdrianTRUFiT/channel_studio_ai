# Manual Checklist: Before Launching Your YouTube Video

*(Kid/team-friendly – follow this after your video is fully edited and exported as MP4. Designed for faceless videos like your AI Governance series.)*

This is **Step 7+** from the earlier video creation workflow. Do these steps in YouTube Studio before hitting “Publish.” It takes 15–30 minutes and makes a huge difference in views.

**Super Simple Pre-Launch 8-Step Process**

**Step 1: Final Video Check (On Your Computer)**

- Watch the full video one last time.
- Check: Under 5 minutes? Clear message? Good sound and pictures?
- Fix any mistakes and **re-export** as MP4 (1080p is perfect).
- Rename the file with your main keyword, e.g., AI-Authorization-Governance-Explained.mp4.

**Step 2: Prepare Thumbnail (The Click Magnet)**

- Use **Canva** (free and easy for kids).
- Size: 1280 × 720 pixels.
- Make it bright, simple, and readable on phones: Big text (e.g., “What is AI Authorization?”), relevant picture (lock/shield), your channel colors.
- Save as JPG or PNG.
- Test: Does it look good small? Would *you* click it?

**Step 3: Write Title (Most Important!)**

- Keep it under 60–70 characters.
- Put main keyword near the front.
- Make it interesting: “AI Authorization Explained: How AI Decides Who Gets Access (Simple Guide)”
- Rules: Honest, no clickbait, creates curiosity.

**Step 4: Write Description (The SEO Helper)**

- First 1–2 lines: Hook + what the video teaches (this shows in search results).
- Add timestamps (chapters) like:
0:00 Intro
0:45 What is Authorization?
2:10 Why It Matters
- Include keywords naturally.
- Add links (your other videos, website).
- End with call-to-action: “Like and subscribe for more AI tips!”
- Aim for 200+ words if possible.

**Step 5: Add Tags**

- 8–15 tags.
- Start with exact main keyword.
- Add variations: “AI governance”, “AI authorization explained”, “how AI access control works”, etc.
- Include broad ones like “artificial intelligence”, “education”.

**Step 6: Gather Extras**

- **Subtitles/Captions**: Use YouTube’s auto-captions and edit them (important for education videos).
- **End Screen**: Add “Subscribe”, link to another video.
- **Cards**: Pop-up links during the video (optional but good).
- Choose **Playlist** to add it to (e.g., “AI Governance Series”).

**Step 7: Upload & Fill in YouTube Studio**

1. Go to YouTube Studio → **Create** → **Upload videos**.
2. Drag your MP4 file.
3. While uploading: Fill Title, Description, Thumbnail, Tags.
4. Set **Category** → Education (best for this topic).
5. Audience: “Not made for kids” (usually).
6. Visibility: Start as **Unlisted** or **Private** to check everything.

**Step 8: Final Review & Go Live**

- Wait for full processing (check 1080p quality).
- Preview on phone and computer.
- Double-check: Everything correct? No spelling mistakes?
- Set to **Public** (or **Schedule** for best time).
- Optional: Premier it for live chat excitement.
- Pin a comment (e.g., “What do you want to learn next about AI?”).

**Quick Kid/Team Roles for This Phase**

- **Thumbnail Kid**: Designs in Canva.
- **Writer Kid**: Does title + description.
- **SEO Checker**: Adds tags and timestamps.
- **Reviewer**: Watches preview and says “Ready!”

**Why These Steps Matter**

- **Title + Thumbnail** = clicks.
- **Description + Tags** = YouTube finds your video.
- Checking everything = fewer mistakes and better algorithm push.
