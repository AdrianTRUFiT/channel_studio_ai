# Manual Process for Day Trading

*(Kid-friendly checklist style – effective basics that a team can follow step-by-step. Start with paper trading only!)*

**Important Warning**

Day trading is **risky** — most people lose money. Always start with **paper trading** (fake money) for months. Never use money you can’t afford to lose. This is for learning and simulation.

**Goal**

Follow a repeatable daily process to practice **momentum/breakout day trading** on stocks. Aim to make small, consistent simulated profits while strictly protecting capital. Focus on 1–3 high-quality trades per day.

**The Super Simple 7-Step Daily Process**

**Step 1: Pre-Market Prep (Before Market Opens)**

- Wake up early (e.g., 30–60 minutes before open).
- Check news: Use free sites like Yahoo Finance, Finviz, or CNBC for big movers or company news.
- Scan for stocks: Look at **% Gainers** (stocks up a lot pre-market with good volume).
- Build a **Watchlist** of 5–10 stocks that are moving strongly (high volume, clear trend).
- Mark key levels on charts: Previous day high/low, round numbers.
- Set your **Daily Rules**: Max risk per trade = 1% of account. Daily loss limit = 2–3%.

**Step 2: Choose Your Setup (The Strategy)**

Use one simple pattern (e.g., **First Pullback** or **Opening Range Breakout**):

- Stock gaps up strongly.
- It pulls back a bit but holds support (like VWAP or moving average).
- Volume is high.
- Wait for confirmation (price starts moving up again).
Only take “A+” setups that match your checklist. Skip everything else.

**Step 3: Plan the Trade (Before Entering)**

For every potential trade, write down or say out loud:

- Entry price
- Stop-loss (where you exit if wrong — keep risk at 1%)
- Target (aim for at least 2x your risk, e.g., risk $100 to make $200+)
- Position size (how many shares: calculate so you only risk 1%)

**Formula example**: Shares = (Account Risk $) ÷ (Entry Price – Stop Price)

**Step 4: Execute the Trade**

- During market hours, watch your watchlist.
- Enter only when setup triggers.
- Immediately set stop-loss order.
- Take partial profits (e.g., sell half at first target).
- Let the rest run with a trailing stop if it keeps going.

**Step 5: Manage & Exit**

- Stick to your plan — no moving stops out of hope.
- Exit at target or stop.
- If you hit daily loss limit → **Stop trading for the day**. Walk away.

**Step 6: Review & Journal (Right After Market or End of Day)**

- Save screenshots of every trade.
- Answer: What worked? What didn’t? Did I follow rules?
- Calculate win rate and P&L for the day.
- Save in a “Completed Trades” folder or simple spreadsheet.

**Step 7: Improve (Weekly)**

- Look at all trades from the week.
- Adjust one thing (e.g., better scanning).
- Celebrate following the process (even if P&L is small).

**Skills vs Tools Matching (Simple Version)**

**Skills needed:**

- Reading charts & news
- Following rules strictly (discipline)
- Quick math for position size & risk
- Journaling & reviewing
- Patience (waiting for good setups)

**Tools (All Free or Low-Cost):**

- **Charts & Scanner**: TradingView (free), Finviz
- **Paper Trading**: Thinkorswim (paperMoney), Webull, TradingView Paper Trading
- **News**: Yahoo Finance, CNBC
- **Journal**: Google Sheets or notebook
- **Calculator**: Phone calculator for risk math

**How to Run This with a Team of Kids (or Beginners)**

1. **Roles** (rotate daily):
    - Scanner/News Hunter
    - Chart Analyst (marks levels)
    - Risk Calculator
    - Trade Executor (in simulator)
    - Reviewer/Journaler
2. Use a big printed checklist on the wall. Check off each step. Use stickers for completed days.
3. **Practice Routine**: Start with 1-hour sessions. Do paper trading only. Review as a group.
4. Keep a “Progress Wall” showing simulated account growth over time.

**Why This Works for Beginners**

- **One simple strategy** instead of many.
- Heavy emphasis on **risk rules** (this is what keeps you alive).
- Every step is repeatable and checklist-driven.
- Visual + team-based = engaging.
- Builds real skills through repetition and review.
- Scalable: Master paper trading → small real account later (with adult supervision).

**Print this** and tape it next to your computer. Do 20–30 practice days before changing anything.
