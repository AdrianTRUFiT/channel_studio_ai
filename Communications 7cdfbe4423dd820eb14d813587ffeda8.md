# Communications

Here’s how to treat communication as a first‑class part of the fulfillment pipeline, not an afterthought.

---

1. Communication objectives in fulfillment
- Clarity: buyer knows exactly what they bought, what they received, and what happens next.
• Confidence: it feels intentional and premium, not improvised.
• Continuity: every message positions the next step in your system (upsell, engagement, renewal).

---

1. Communication surfaces in the pipeline

Stage: Delivery prep

- Artifacts:• Delivery message: “Here’s what you now have + how to start.”
• Instructions: step‑by‑step, minimal friction.
• Success path: what “winning” looks like with this offer.

Stage: Delivery execution

- Channel choices:• Email (primary record + links).
• Portal/workspace (for assets).
• Optional SMS/DM for “you’re in” confirmation.

Stage: Confirmation

- Artifacts:• Receipt: what was delivered, when, and where.
• Anchor link: one canonical place to return to (portal, doc, or page).

---

1. Tone and positioning
- Voice: confident, direct, non‑needy.
• Positioning: “You’ve entered a system,” not “Here’s a random product.”
• Framing: always tie the delivery to the broader engine—
“This is step 1 in [Outcome X]. Next, you’ll see [Y]. After that, we’ll move into [Z].”

---

1. Where it lives in your architecture
- Templates:• /monetization/delivery/templates/ for reusable message structures.
- Per‑buyer artifacts:• /monetization/delivery/{buyer_id}/delivery_message.md
• /monetization/delivery/{buyer_id}/instructions.md
- System‑level logic:• Communication is generated by a Messaging/Onboarding agent inside the fulfillment pipeline, not manually.
